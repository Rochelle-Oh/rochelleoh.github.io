1. YouTube Origins

I used to make YouTube videos! Here are three of my best / most recent videos. The first two are lookbooks (oh how I loved a lookbook!) with dynamic edits and no voiceover. The last is a studio vlog that is a longer-form and chatty, documenting about a few weeks of footage. With my videos, I always tried to have a balance between aesthetics, humour, earnestness and play, no matter the type of video it was. They were made to be mood-setting, comforting and inspirational, and I was always trying to do something different or unexpected. Editing was the part of the process I was most confident in; if I had no idea what I should document or couldn't properly explain my point or accidentally shot a whole scene out of focus, I could rely on my editing skills to pull the threads together. As they say, we'll fix it in post!
2. Professional Working Woman

Once I started working for not-for-profit label Music in Exile, I got to edit some campaign content for them in-between designing record covers and posters! Here are two behind-the-scenes interviews I edited. ~
❁ The first is a relaxed, informal interview with Maryos Syawish and Corey Kikos of Sleep D and Butter Sessions, discussing the process of creating their remix of Gordon Koang's track 'Y Dah'.
❁ The second is a shorter social promo with Gordon and Paul Biel at non-profit fashion school The Social Studio, where a collaborative flag and custom suits were being designed for the musicians.
Both videos were shot with on the fly by the wonderful Nick McKinlay. With two people speaking in stream of consciousness in both videos, the editing pace was largely informed by the artists' natural speaking pace, leaving time for for thinking and processing while cutting out the um's and ah's. The diegetic music in the first video added an extra challenge to subtely condensing and rearranging different key thoughts and moments into a cohesive story 🤔
