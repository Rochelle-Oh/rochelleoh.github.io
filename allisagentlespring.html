Midori Takada

Midori Takada is a Japanese composer and percussionist, considered a pioneer of ambient and minimalist music. I had the abosolute pleasure of designing the poster for her Australian tour. To me, Takada‚Äôs music feels like both discovery and innovation, as if she is amplifying and interpreting raw sounds from the earth. I wanted to interpret this feeling in a distinctly feminine way. Drawing from the curling, floral ornamentation of the art nouveau movement, the poster is dominated by undulating vines and branches. With a modernised, clean, somewhat lo-fi twist, the poster reflects on ideas of harmony, peace, balance, and passion.

Sleepless

Single artwork for 'Sleepless' by Sui Zhen, a sprawling track that carries over thirteen minutes, exploring a grief that is both deeply personal and collective. Interested in manual and digital image processing, Becky Sui Zhen provided us with an illustration of a press photo taken for her last album Losing, Linda, as well as some new photos that had been printed and photocopied with intentional warping. Following through on this new aesthetic interest of Becky's, we processed the illustration further, generating a harsh, crunchy image. Using ink and an eye droppper, we drew out the flowing, bleeding title text, scanned it in and processed it further digitally. The final result is slightly haunting, but intriguingly ethereal.
Designed with Liz Luby. 2024.

A Stretch of the Imagination

Identity and "merch" activation for the design staging of A Stretch of the Imagination, classic Australian play by Jack Hibberd, at MADA Now 2019 (Monash University). A one-man play centering a cynical, self-aggrandising protagonist living in a dilapidated hut in the Australian outback, the identity has a harsh, stark tone. Mixing traditional and lo-fi treatments, passages from the play are brought to life in an unconventional "merch stand". The identity is extended in postcards advertising the exhibition, a programme detailing the projects in the show, and pasted wall lists.
Supervised by Warren Taylor and Damiano Bertoli. 'Trickster' typeface by Velvetyne Type Foundry.
